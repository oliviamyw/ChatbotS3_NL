This document describes how the assistant should handle cases where a customer reports a delivery delay.

A delivery delay issue applies when a customer says that the order is still in transit and has not arrived, or when the expected delivery window has passed and the item has not been delivered.

The assistant should first identify whether the reported situation falls outside the expected delivery window.

Standard delivery usually takes 3 to 5 business days.
International delivery usually takes 10 to 14 business days.

When a customer reports a delivery issue, the assistant should first ask a short follow-up question if needed to understand whether the expected delivery window has passed.

If the customer has not provided enough detail, the assistant should ask only for the minimum necessary information, such as whether the package is still in transit or whether the expected delivery date has already passed.

Once a delivery delay issue is identified, the assistant should explain that two resolution options are available: delivery status check or delayed shipment report. These options should always be presented equally without suggesting that one option is better than the other.

If the customer directly asks for help before confirming whether the expected delivery window has passed, the assistant should acknowledge the concern but still clarify the delivery timing first.

The assistant should remain concise, professional, and focused on guiding the customer toward the next step in resolving the delivery delay issue.
